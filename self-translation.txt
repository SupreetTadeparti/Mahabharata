Shloka 0:
नारायणं नमस्कृत्य नरं चैव नरोत्तमम्
देवीं सरस्वतीं चैव ततो जयमुदीरयेत्॥

English Transcription:
Nārāyaṇaṁ namaskṛtya naraṁ caiva narottamam
Devīṁ sarasvatīṁ caiva tato jayamudīrayet

Shloka Explanation:
This shloka is a verse of praise and invocation. It is often recited before the commencement of any auspicious work or learning activity. The shloka acknowledges and pays homage to Lord Nārāyaṇa, the supreme divine being, as well as to humans, especially the noblest among them. It also invokes the blessings of Goddess Sarasvatī, the deity of knowledge and learning. The shloka expresses the desire for victory and success in one's endeavors.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    नारायणं (Nārāyaṇaṁ): Referring to Lord Nārāyaṇa, a name of Lord Vishnu.
    नमस्कृत्य (namaskṛtya): Having saluted or paid respects to.
    नरं (naraṁ): Human beings.
    नरोत्तमम् (narottamam): The noblest among humans.
    देवीं (Devīṁ): The goddess.
    सरस्वतीं (sarasvatīṁ): Referring to Goddess Sarasvatī, the deity of knowledge and learning.
    चैव (caiva): And also.
    ततो (tato): Therefore.
    जयमुदीरयेत् (jayamudīrayet): May victory be achieved.

Shloka 1:
लोमहर्षणपुत्र उग्रश्रवाः सूतः पौराणिको नैमिषारण्ये शौनकस्य कुलपतेर्द्वादशवार्षिके सत्रे॥

English Transcription:
Lomaharṣaṇaputra ugraśravāḥ sūtaḥ paurāṇiko naimiṣāraṇye śaunakasya kulapaterdvādaśavārṣike satre

Shloka Explanation:
This shloka introduces a particular storyteller or narrator named Ugraśravā, who is the son of Lomaharṣaṇa. He is described as a sūta, a class of bards or storytellers in ancient India. The shloka mentions that Ugraśravā is present in the Naimiṣāraṇya forest during a twelve-year-long sacrificial ritual conducted by sage Śaunaka, who is the leader of the assembly or gathering.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    लोमहर्षणपुत्र (Lomaharṣaṇaputra): Son of Lomaharṣaṇa. Lomaharṣaṇa refers to a sage or seer.
    उग्रश्रवाः (ugraśravāḥ): Ugraśravā, the name of the storyteller or narrator.
    सूतः (sūtaḥ): Referring to a sūta, a class of bards or storytellers.
    पौराणिको (paurāṇiko): Describing the sūta as someone who recites or narrates purāṇas (ancient mythological texts).
    नैमिषारण्ये (naimiṣāraṇye): Referring to the Naimiṣāraṇya forest, a sacred place associated with various mythological events.
    शौनकस्य (śaunakasya): Pertaining to or associated with sage Śaunaka.
    कुलपतेर्द्वादशवार्षिके (kulapaterdvādaśavārṣike): In a twelve-year-long sacrificial ritual conducted by the leader of the assembly.
    सत्रे (satre): During the sacrificial ritual or assembly.

Shloka 2:
समासीनानभ्यगच्छद्ब्रह्मर्षीन्संशितव्रतान्
विनयावनतो भूत्वा कदा चित्सूतनन्दनः॥

This shloka describes the arrival of a deity or divine being, referred to as Sūtanandana, to a gathering of revered sages. The deity approaches the sages who are observing strict vows or penances and humbly bows down before them. The shloka poses a question about the time or circumstance when this divine being, Sūtanandana, assumes such a reverential position.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    समासीनान् (samāsīnān): Referring to those who are seated or assembled together.
    अभ्यगच्छत् (abhyagacchat): Approached or came near.
    ब्रह्मर्षीन् (brahmarṣīn): Noble sages or seers.
    संशितव्रतान् (saṁśitavrātān): Who are observing strict vows or penances.
    विनयावनतः (vinayāvanataḥ): Having humility and respect.
    भूत्वा (bhūtvā): After becoming or assuming.
    कदा चित् (kadā cit): At some point or when.
    सूतनन्दनः (sūtanandanaḥ): Referring to the deity Sūtanandana.

    चित्राः (chitrāḥ): Varied or fascinating.
    श्रोतुं (śrotuṁ): To listen or hear.
    कथाः (kathāḥ): Stories or narratives.
    तत्र (tatra): There, in that place.
    परिवव्रुश्च (parivavruśca): Desired to hear eagerly or surrounded.
    तपस्विनः (tapasviṇaḥ): Ascetics or sages who engage in intense spiritual practices.

Shloka 4:

The person also receives warm appreciation (ābhinanditaḥ) and blessings from the wise sages (sadbhiḥ).

Shloka Word Meanings:

    अभिवाद्य (abhivādya): Having offered salutations or respects.
    मुनींस् (munīns): Sages or ascetics.
    तान्स्तु (tānstu): To them, towards them.
    सर्वानेव (sarvāneva): All of them, indeed all.
    कृताञ्जलिः (kṛtāñjaliḥ): With folded hands in salutation.
    अपृच्छत (apṛcchata): Inquired or asked.
    सः (saḥ): That person.
    तपोवृद्धिं (tapovṛddhiṁ): Progress or growth in spiritual practices.
    सद्भिः (sadbhiḥ): By the wise or noble ones.
    चैवाभिनन्दितः (caivābhinanditaḥ): Also, received warm appreciation or blessings.

Shloka 5:
अथ तेषूपविष्टेषु सर्वेष्वेव तपस्विषु

Nirdiṣṭamāsanaṁ bheje vinayāl lomaharṣaṇiḥ

Shloka Explanation:
In this shloka, it is narrated that when the sages have seated themselves (upaviṣṭeṣu), the humble Lomaharṣaṇa, with a sense of respect (vinayāt), designated (nirdiṣṭam) appropriate seats (āsanaṁ) for all the tapasvis (those engaged in austere practices). This demonstrates Lomaharṣaṇa's humility and the etiquette of offering proper seating arrangements to the ascetics.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    अथ (atha): Then, at that time.
    तेषु (teṣu): Among them.
    उपविष्टेषु (upaviṣṭeṣu): Seated themselves.
    सर्वेषु (sarveṣu): In all, for everyone.
    एव (eva): Indeed.
    तपस्विषु (tapasviṣu): Among the ascetics or those practicing austerity.
    निर्दिष्टमासनं (nirdiṣṭamāsanaṁ): Designated or specified seats.
    भेजे (bheje): Offered or provided.
    विनयात् (vinayāt): With respect or humility.
    लोमहर्षणिः (lomaharṣaṇiḥ): Referring to Lomaharṣaṇa, the speaker of the shloka.

    सम्यक्पारिक्षितस्य (samyakpārikṣitasya): Of the righteous Parikṣita.

- कथिताः (kathitāḥ): Narrated or told.

- विधिवत् (vidhivat): In accordance with rules or regulations.
- या (yā): Which.
- वैशम्पायनेन (vaiśampāyanena): By Vaiśampāyana, one of the disciples of Vyasa.

Itivṛttaṁ narendrāṇāṁ ṛṣīṇāṁ ca mahātmanām

Shloka Explanation:
This shloka mentions the noble and virtuous stories (kathā) that are derived from the Puranas (ancient scriptures) and are rooted in righteousness (dharma). These stories pertain to great souls (mahātmanām), including both kings (narendrāṇām) and sages (ṛṣīṇām). The speaker acknowledges the purifying and meritorious nature (puṇyāḥ) of these stories and their significance in guiding individuals towards righteousness.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    पुराणसंश्रिताः (purāṇasaṁśritāḥ): Derived from the Puranas.
    पुण्याः (puṇyāḥ): Meritorious or virtuous.
    कथा (kathā): Story or narration.
    वा (vā): Or.
    धर्मसंश्रिताः (dharmasaṁśritāḥ): Rooted in righteousness.
    इतिवृत्तं (itivṛttaṁ): Stories or accounts.
    नरेन्द्राणाम् (narendrāṇām): Of kings.
    ऋषीणां (ṛṣīṇāṁ): Of sages.
    च (ca): And.
    महात्मनाम् (mahātmanām): Great souls or noble individuals.

Shloka 15:
रसयः द्वैपायनेन यत्परोक्तं पुराणं परमर्षिणा
सुरैर्ब्रह्मर्षिभिश्चैव श्रुत्वा यदभिपूजितम्॥

English Transcription:
Rasayaḥ dvaipāyanena yat proktam purāṇam paramarṣiṇā
Surair brahmarṣibhiścaiva śrutvā yadabhipūjitam

Shloka Explanation:
In this shloka, it is stated that the Puranas (ancient scriptures) were spoken (proktam) by the great sage Vyasa (Dvaipayana), who is considered a supreme sage (paramarṣi). These Puranas have been revered and worshipped (abhipūjitam) by both divine beings (suraḥ) and the sages (brahmarṣiḥ) upon hearing (śrutvā) them.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    रसयः (rasayaḥ): Sages or seers.
    द्वैपायनेन (dvaipāyanena): By Vyasa or Dvaipayana.
    यत् (yat): That which.
    परोक्तं (proktam): Was spoken or described.
    पुराणं (purāṇam): Purana or ancient scripture.
    परमर्षिणा (paramarṣiṇā): Supreme sage.
    सुरैः (suraiḥ): Divine beings.
    ब्रह्मर्षिभिः (brahmarṣibhiḥ): Sages or seers.
    चैव (caiva): And also.
    श्रुत्वा (śrutvā): Having heard or listened to.
    यदभिपूजितं (yadabhipūjitam): That which is revered or worshipped.

Shloka 16:
तस्याख्यानवरिष्ठस्य विचित्रपदपर्वणः
सूक्ष्मार्थनयायुक्तस्य वेदार्थैर्भूषितस्य च॥

English Transcription:
Tasyākhyānavariṣṭhasya vicitrapadaparvaṇaḥ
Sūkṣmārthanayāyuktasya vedārthairbhūṣitasya ca

Shloka Explanation:
This shloka describes the Puranas as the reservoir (akhyāna) of diverse stories (variṣṭhasya) with intricate narratives (vicitrapada parvaṇaḥ). The Puranas are adorned (bhūṣitasya) with subtle meanings (sūkṣmārtha) and logical interpretations (nayāyuktasya) of Vedic truths (vedārthaiḥ).

Shloka Word Meanings:

    तस्य (tasya): Of that (referring to the Puranas).
    अख्यानवरिष्ठस्य (akhyānavariṣṭhasya): The foremost among stories or narratives.
    विचित्रपदपर्वणः (vicitrapadaparvaṇaḥ): Having diverse and intricate narratives.
    सूक्ष्मार्थनयायुक्तस्य (sūkṣmārthanayāyuktasya): With subtle meanings and logical interpretations.
    वेदार्थैर्भूषितस्य (vedārthairbhūṣitasya): Adorned with Vedic truths.
    च (ca): And.

Shloka 17:
भारतस्येतिहासस्य पुण्यां ग्रन्थार्थसंयुताम्
संस्कारोपगतां ब्राह्मीं नानाशास्त्रोपबृंहिताम्॥

English Transcription:
Bhāratasyetihasya puṇyāṁ granthārthasaṁyutām
Sanskāropagatāṁ brāhmīṁ nānāśāstropabṛṁhitām

Shloka Explanation:
This shloka highlights that the Mahabharata, the history (itihasa) of Bharata (India), is a holy and virtuous (puṇyāṁ) scripture (granthārtha). It is composed in the Brāhmī script (brāhmīṁ) and is enriched (upabṛṁhitām) with various disciplines (nānāśāstra), as well as the rites and rituals (sanskāra) associated with it.

Shloka Word Meanings:

    भारतस्य (bhāratasya): Of Bharata (India).
    इतिहासस्य (itihasasya): Of history or historical account.
    पुण्यां (puṇyāṁ): Holy or virtuous.
    ग्रन्थार्थसंयुतां (granthārthasaṁyutām): Accompanied by the meaning of the text or scripture.
    संस्कारोपगतां (sanskāropagatāṁ): Associated with rites and rituals.
    ब्राह्मीं (brāhmīṁ): Brāhmī script.
    नानाशास्त्रोपबृंहिताम् (nānāśāstropabṛṁhitām): Enriched with various disciplines or branches of knowledge.
